5 Essential Vitamins Every Liberian Should Take Daily

June 15, 2025 · Ducor International Pharmacy · 5 min read

Living in Liberia's tropical climate puts unique demands on your body. Heat, humidity, tropical diseases, and dietary habits all affect what your body needs to stay healthy. While a balanced diet is the ideal source of nutrients, most people benefit from daily vitamin supplements to fill the gaps. Here are the five most important ones.

1. Vitamin C — Your Immune Shield

Vitamin C is one of the most powerful antioxidants your body needs. It helps your immune system fight off infections — from the common cold to more serious tropical illnesses. In a country where infectious diseases are common, keeping your immune system strong is essential.

Vitamin C also helps your body absorb iron (see below), heals wounds faster, and protects your skin from damage. Adults need at least 75–90mg per day.

Good food sources in Liberia: Oranges, mangoes, pineapple, tomatoes, and pepper. If your diet is low in these, a daily Vitamin C supplement is a smart choice.

2. Vitamin D — Not Just From Sunshine

Many Liberians assume they get enough Vitamin D because of the sunshine — but research shows that most people in West Africa are still deficient. This is because much of the time spent outdoors is in shade, and skin tone affects how much Vitamin D your body produces.

Vitamin D is essential for strong bones, a healthy immune system, and good mood. Low Vitamin D has been linked to fatigue, bone pain, and increased risk of infections.

Who is most at risk? People who work indoors, women who cover their skin fully, and children who spend most time inside.

3. Iron — Fighting Anaemia

Anaemia (low iron in the blood) is extremely common in Liberia, especially among women, pregnant mothers, and young children. Iron is what your red blood cells need to carry oxygen around your body. When you are low on iron, you feel constantly tired, weak, and short of breath.

Women lose iron every month through menstruation. Pregnant women need significantly more. Parasitic infections like malaria and hookworm also deplete iron levels.

Important: Do not take iron supplements without confirming you are deficient first — too much iron can be harmful. Ask our pharmacist for guidance.

4. Folic Acid — Critical for Women

Folic Acid (Vitamin B9) is essential for every woman of childbearing age, especially during the first three months of pregnancy. It prevents serious birth defects that affect the baby's brain and spine.

Even if you are not pregnant, folic acid helps your body produce healthy red blood cells and supports brain function. It works closely with iron to prevent anaemia.

Recommendation: All women planning to get pregnant, or who could become pregnant, should take 400mcg of Folic Acid every day.

5. Zinc — Healing and Immunity

Zinc is involved in over 300 processes in the body, yet many Liberians do not get enough from their diet. It supports your immune system, helps wounds heal faster, improves skin health, and is especially important for children's growth and development.

Zinc deficiency is common in areas where the diet is heavy in plant foods and light on meat. Signs include slow wound healing, frequent infections, loss of taste or smell, and poor growth in children.

Children benefit greatly from zinc — it reduces the severity of diarrhoea and respiratory infections, two of the leading causes of childhood illness in Liberia.

How to Take Vitamins Safely

  • Take vitamins with food — this improves absorption and reduces stomach upset
  • Do not take more than the recommended dose thinking "more is better" — some vitamins are harmful in high doses
  • If you are on medication for any condition, check with our pharmacist before starting supplements
  • Store vitamins in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight
